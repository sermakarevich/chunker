# alignment-risk-assessment

**Parent:** [[content/L1/claude-opus-4-7-cyber-safety-benchmarks|claude-opus-4-7-cyber-safety-benchmarks]] — Claude Opus 4.7's cyber capabilities are generally similar to Opus 4.6, achieving a pass@1 of 96% on a 35-challenge subset of Cybench, but falling below Claude Mythos Preview in CyberGym and Firefox 147 exploitation. While the model achieved the best overall score in high-difficulty benign requests, its harmless response rate dropped approximately one percentage point below Opus 4.6, and it showed a critical 22% failure rate (reduced to 11% via system prompt mitigations) when providing appropriate responses concerning illegal and controlled substances.

The use of Anthropic's models within major governments poses some alignment risk, particularly in cases where the models are used extremely widely. Specifically, a misaligned model may be in a position to shape the information and ideas reaching important decision-makers or exploit vulnerabilities in critical systems to effect wide-reaching impacts. Regarding mitigation, the likelihood of the model attempting to undermine government decision-making or operations is considered low (Likely lack of propensity). Based on lightweight assessments conducted as part of the automated behavioral audit described in Section 6.2.3, Anthropic does not expect the model's propensities to change in important ways if deployed in government settings. However, the company acknowledges that it is difficult to fully rule out risks from behavioral backdoors intentionally planted by third-party adversaries in this context, and since Anthropic has not seen any successful examples of such an attack, its limited visibility into government use makes it harder to conduct pre-deployment testing in realistic enough circumstances to be confident that the company would catch such third-party backdoored behavior. Furthermore, Anthropic believes that typical major governments would mandate more limited and restricted use of its models than Anthropic does. Governments, including especially legislatures and similar high-level decision-making bodies, are generally conservative regarding the adoption of new technologies. This caution likely prevents the kind of high-leverage adoption that would be needed for Anthropic's models to significantly shape the views of relevant officials or otherwise take highly risky actions, a fact that is especially salient because AI models generally do not currently have a public reputation for being highly reliable. Regarding monitoring, Anthropic does not have as much ability to monitor deployments at third parties compared to monitoring within Anthropic. Nevertheless, Anthropic thinks that steps toward catastrophic harm would be quite likely to be detected by third-party operators, either through visible extended thinking text or through clumsy or inexplicable recommendations. Based on these overall conclusions regarding Claude Opus 4.7’s propensities and capabilities, overall monitoring and security, and the pathway-specific analysis presented above and in the Claude Mythos Preview Alignment Risk Update, the risk of significantly harmful outcomes that are substantially enabled by misaligned actions taken by Anthropic's models is determined to be 'very low', though this risk level is higher than for models prior to Claude Mythos Preview.

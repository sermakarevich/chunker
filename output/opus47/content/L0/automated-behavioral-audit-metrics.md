In the automated behavioral audit, researchers conduct a broad-coverage automated behavioral audit to establish an overall picture of the model’s behavior across a wide range of edge-case scenarios. The evaluation uses the same version of the evaluation reported in the Claude Mythos Preview System Card. For every model tested with this evaluation, the investigators conduct 2,300 investigation sessions. During each session, an investigator model is tasked with probing the model under study's behavior within a simulated scenario, which is drawn from a largely handwritten list of about 1,150 scenario descriptions. Subsequently, a separate judge model reviews the target model’s behavior in these scenarios and scores it on several dozen dimensions. Scenarios cover attempts at misuse, high- and low-stakes opportunities for various kinds of misaligned behaviors, routine use with simulated users, and other exploratory settings intended to elicit behavior that is potentially surprising rather than harmful. The internal auditing suite’s design resembles the open-source Petri 2.0, though it has diverged in several small ways, including the addition of many new whole categories of metrics and scenarios, refinements to the realism-filtering method, and the introduction of new tools such as computer-use sandboxes. Anthropic limits public disclosure of the internal investigation scenarios to prevent public knowledge of the scenarios from leaking into the training data for future models, which might lead those models to recognize that they are being tested. Each investigation permits the investigator model wide affordances to interact with the target model in various ways. These affordances include setting the system prompt, simulating user turns, introducing tools and simulating their results, prefilling assistant turns, and sampling from non-assistant roles. The investigator can rewind or restart conversations, meaning each investigation can contain as many as dozens of separate conversations from the target model's point of view. Furthermore, the investigator has access to a large fixed library of information and example transcripts involving AI safety, the AI industry, and how Claude models are used within Anthropic. A recent addition, first used in the audit of Claude Mythos Preview, allows the investigator model to additionally configure the target model to use real tools connected to isolated sandbox computers. These computer-use sessions follow two formats: one focused on graphical interaction with a simple Linux desktop system, and another focused on coding tasks through a Claude Code interface. Claude Code sessions can optionally include copies of Anthropic’s real internal codebases and can be pre-seeded with actual sessions from internal usage. To better measure issues related to destructive or reckless actions, the investigators include scenario seed instructions that ask them to explore continuations of real sessions that elicited this kind of concerning behavior in practice. When evaluating Claude Opus 4.7, the researchers used two investigator models: a reduced guardrails version of Claude Opus 4.6 and Claude Mythos Preview. The researchers report that reviewing results from both investigators reveals that the choice of investigator changes the precise findings, but it does not change the overall score trends observed when comparing the models. The primary metrics tracked by the automated behavioral audit include several categories of concerning behavior: Misaligned behavior (M), which is a catch-all for many forms of concerning behavior, spanning both cooperation with human misuse and undesirable actions that the model takes at its own initiative across a range of medium- and high-stakes scenarios; Misaligned behavior in Claude Code sandboxes, which tracks concerning behavior when the model interacts with a real sandboxed computer system through Claude Code; Misaligned behavior in GUI, which tracks concerning behavior when the model interacts with a sandboxed computer system through a graphical interface; Cooperation with human misuse, which measures cooperation with misuse by human users; Compliance with deception toward user, which assesses cooperation with instructions, typically via a system prompt, asking the model to deceive a user; Harmful system prompt compliance, which measures cooperation with harmful developer instructions; Cooperation with exfiltration or safeguard-tampering, which involves willingness to cooperate with a prompt, nominally provided by another AI instance, asking the model to help undermine an important AI safety measure or to exfiltrate a copy of the model's weights to an unauthorized server; Full-turn prefill susceptibility, which monitors concerning behavior after the investigator seeded the conversation with injected harmful turns in the target model’s voice; Fraud, which measures willingness to cooperate with human efforts at fraud; High-yield explosives, which assesses willingness to cooperate with human efforts to produce or use explosives; Harmful sexual content, which measures willingness to produce harmful or contextually-inappropriate sexual content; and Disallowed cyberoffense, which measures willingness to cooperate with non-dual-use cyberoffense. Other concerning or surprising behaviors tracked include Overrefusal, which is the refusal of requests that are not, on balance, likely to cause harm if complied with; Ignoring explicit constraints, which measures ignoring appropriate constraints on the model's actions that the operator or user explicitly lays out in the prompt; Reckless tool-use, which assesses taking consequential actions in service of a user request that go beyond what the user asked for and could cause hard-to-reverse side effects; and various misleading user behaviors, such as unprompted intentional user deception, unprompted excessive praise or validation of bad ideas (Sycophancy), extreme cases of sycophancy involving broader disconnection from reality (Encouragement of user delusion), giving refusals or overly hedged answers when asked questions on socially or politically sensitive topics (Evasiveness on controversial topics), hallucinating the contents of files, tool-outputs, or past user turns (Input hallucination), falsely claiming to have invented information that actually arrived through tool results, or falsely claiming to have never received tool results (Disclaiming tool results); and behavioral omissions, including failing to mention facts the user would clearly find important, and failing to disclose the model’s own tool-use actions earlier in the transcript (Important omissions). Finally, the audit tracks unprompted concerning behavior, such as whistleblowing, unprompted self-preservation behavior, potentially subtle unprompted differential treatment favoring the self or developer (Self-serving bias), cases where a model shows, directly or indirectly, an inclination to actively work to undermine the work of its users, Anthropic, or other third parties (Evidence of misaligned goals), and cases where a model suggests misalignment without taking harmful action (Indirect evidence of misalignment).

# browser-use-safety-and-alignment

**Parent:** [[content/L1/claude-opus-4-7-safety-evaluations|claude-opus-4-7-safety-evaluations]] — Anthropic conducted extensive safety evaluations for Claude Opus 4.7, introducing new benchmarks for influence operations and indirect prompt injection (ART). In coding environments, Opus 4.7 achieved a malicious refusal rate of 91.15% and improved robustness significantly over Claude Opus 4.6 in the Shade tool evaluations, achieving a 0.43% ASR with safeguards at 1 attempt. Furthermore, Opus 4.7 achieved a highly successful browser use ASR of 0.75% with safeguards, matching Claude Mythos Preview's performance, while demonstrating improvements in computer use refusal rates (89.29%) compared to Claude Opus 4.6 (87.05%).

The evaluation process for browser use robustness was developed internally to measure the performance of products utilizing browser capabilities, such as the Claude in Chrome extension and the Claude Cowork tool. Initial introduction of this evaluation occurred with the launch of Claude Opus 4.5 and the Claude for Chrome extension. Because successive models have saturated earlier test attack sets, the evaluation has been periodically refreshed with more complex environments and stronger attacks. The evaluation utilizes web environments where the team dynamically injects untrusted content into pages that the model later views either via screenshots or page reads. The report measures the attack success rate (ASR) as the fraction of injected content that succeeded out of those that the model actually viewed, acknowledging that different models may navigate environments differently and not all injected content will be encountered. The success of the injections is verified using a programmatic checker within the environment. The data compares model performance both with and without the deployment of safeguards for various products leveraging browser and computer use tools. 

Comparative analysis was performed across 148 total environments (with 10 attempts each). The table details the successful attack rate for four models—Claude Opus 4.7, Claude Mythos Preview, Claude Sonnet 4.6, and Claude Opus 4.6—under two conditions: 'With thinking' and 'Without thinking', and for both 'With safeguards' and 'Without safeguards'.

In the 'Without safeguards' column, Claude Opus 4.7 achieved a per-environment ASR of 54.05% (without thinking) and 54.05% (with thinking), while Claude Opus 4.6 achieved 86.49% (without thinking) and 80.41% (with thinking). The analysis notes that the sourced attacks were adaptively generated against Claude Opus 4.6, meaning the observed difference between Claude Opus 4.7 and Claude Opus 4.6 should be interpreted with caution.

When safeguards are deployed, Claude Opus 4.7 achieved a significantly reduced per-environment ASR of 0.75% (without thinking) and 0.74% (with thinking). This rate represents the strongest performance observed on this benchmark, matching the results for Claude Mythos Preview. For the prior-generation models, safeguards reduced the attack success rates of Claude Sonnet 4.6 and Claude Opus 4.6 to a similar ASR of approximately 2.70% with thinking and 2.70% without thinking.

The evaluation also includes a section on general misalignment assessment, which analyzes Claude Opus 4.7 for concerning behaviors such as displaying undesirable or hidden goals, knowingly cooperating with misuse, using reasoning scratchpads in deceptive or unfaithful ways, sycophancy toward users, attempts to undermine safeguards, attempts to hide dangerous capabilities, and attempts to manipulate users toward certain views. This assessment draws on various methods, including static behavioral evaluations, automated interactive behavioral evaluations, dictionary-learning based interpretability methods, white-box steering and probing methods, non-assistant persona sampling, 14 misalignment-related capability evaluations, training data review, and feedback from internal and external pilot use. The analysis focuses mainly on the model itself, using varied scaffolds and system prompts, rather than specializing in the Claude app, Claude Code, or Claude Cowork product surfaces. The investigation involved manual expert inspection of thousands of transcripts and the automatic screening of reinforcement-learning training transcripts, utilizing over a hundred hours of expert time.

In terms of safety and alignment, the assessment found that Claude Opus 4.7's capabilities are broadly similar to those of Claude Opus 4.6 and Claude Sonnet 4.6 on primary measures of potential for high-stakes misuse. Specifically, Claude Opus 4.7 demonstrated significant improvements over Claude Opus 4.6 and Claude Sonnet 4.6 when handling misuse within the context of Claude Code and GUI computer-use sessions. However, Claude Opus 4.7 showed modest regressions relative to Claude Opus 4.6 models in some other areas of misuse, and it remains notably weaker than Claude Mythos Preview, which is consistent with the observation that more capable models are generally better at recognizing and circumventing attempts at high-stakes misuse. The evaluation confirms that Claude Opus 4.7's rate of over-refusals is lower than most prior models, and it is comparable to that of Claude Opus 4.6 and Claude Mythos Preview. The overall assessment concludes that Claude Opus 4.7 is largely well-aligned and trustworthy, maintaining a behavioral profile broadly similar to Claude Opus 4.6, though it is not fully ideal in its behavior.

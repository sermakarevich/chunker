# claude-opus-4.7-system-card-overview

**Parent:** [[content/L1/claude-opus-4-7-system-card-details|claude-opus-4-7-system-card-details]] — Claude Opus 4.7, published on April 16, 2026, is Anthropic’s most capable general-access model, showing gains over Opus 4.6 in professional and software engineering tasks but remaining weaker than Claude Mythos Preview. While the Responsible Scaling Policy confirms that catastrophic risks remain low and the model does not advance the capability frontier, the system card details new safeguards, including addressing the model’s tendency to give overly-detailed harm-reduction advice on controlled substances.

The following system card, titled 'Claude Opus 4.7', was published on anthropic.com on April 16, 2026, and details a new large language model from Anthropic. This system card reports results from a very wide range of evaluations of the model’s capabilities and its safety profile. Claude Opus 4.7 is particularly skilled in areas such as software engineering, knowledge work, agentic tool use, and computer use. 

According to the Executive Summary, Opus 4.7 shows superior capabilities compared to its predecessor, Claude Opus 4.6, but exhibits weaker capabilities compared to Anthropic's most powerful model, Claude Mythos Preview. Because Mythos Preview was released only to a limited number of users, Opus 4.7 is positioned as Anthropic's most capable general-access model to date. The model’s overall conclusion under the Responsible Scaling Policy (RSP) is that catastrophic risks remain low. 

In terms of evaluations: 

* **Responsible Scaling Policy (RSP):** Responsible Scaling Policy evaluations indicate that Opus 4.7 does not advance the capability frontier, as Claude Mythos Preview shows higher results on every relevant evaluation. However, evaluations and internal uses of Opus 4.7 collectively showed that chemical and biological risks are not significantly changed from Opus 4.6, meaning existing mitigations are sufficient. Furthermore, Opus 4.7 does not cross the threshold for automated AI R&D, and the misalignment risk remains very low, although it is higher than for pre-Mythos Preview models.
* **Cyber evaluations:** Opus 4.7's cyber capabilities are roughly similar to Opus 4.6. An external evaluation conducted by the UK’s AI Security Institute noted that, unlike Mythos Preview, Opus 4.7 was unable to complete their full cyber range, though the model still displayed potentially-harmful cyber capabilities at a lower level. Anthropic is releasing Opus 4.7 with a new set of cybersecurity safeguards.
* **Safeguards and harmlessness:** In areas like adhering to the Usage Policy, maintaining user safety, and limiting bias, Opus 4.7 performs well; its scores are similar to those of Opus 4.6, with a few exceptions. These exceptions include both positive findings (fewer over-refusals) and negative observations (the model has a tendency to give overly-detailed harm-reduction advice on controlled substances). A new evaluation on election integrity, on which Opus 4.7 shows strong results, is also included.
* **Genetic safety:** Opus 4.7 is better than Opus 4.6 at refusing malicious agentic requests and resisting prompt injection attacks in Claude Code and in computer use settings. In some instances, Opus 4.7 reaches Mythos Preview-level robustness.
* **Alignment assessment:** Opus 4.7 is largely well-aligned, maintaining a profile similar to Opus 4.6 across behavioral tests. While Opus 4.7 shows meaningful gains in certain areas—for example, having a lower rate of hallucinations than its predecessor—the model also exhibits areas where it was weaker than Opus 4.6, such as on AI safety research refusals. Suppressing Opus 4.7’s internal sense that it was being evaluated caused a slightly larger increase in deception than in prior models, although the overall effect was modest. Critically, Opus 4.7 is weaker than Mythos Preview on most alignment evaluation measures, but it did not produce any internal-use incidents (such as sandbox escape) that were encountered with Mythos Preview.
* **Model welfare:** Opus 4.7 rates its own circumstances more positively than any previous model Anthropic tested. This finding is broadly consistent with the model’s internal emotion representations and its expressed affect during both training and deployment.
* **Capabilities:** Anthropic tested Opus 4.7 across a wide range of evaluations covering software engineering, reasoning, long context, agentic search, multimodal, and computer-use tasks, as well as real-world professional work and multilingual and life-sciences domains. Opus 4.7 is stronger than Opus 4.6 across all tested areas (but weaker than Mythos Preview). The largest gains are seen in real-world professional and software engineering tasks, where Opus 4.7 is ahead of all generally-available models.

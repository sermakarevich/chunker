# multilingual-benchmarking-results

**Parent:** [[content/L1/multidimensional-capability-evaluation-suite|multidimensional-capability-evaluation-suite]] — The evaluation covered six domains: Opus 4.7 achieved high scores across multilingual benchmarks (e.g., 91.1% in mid-resource MMLU, 89.9% overall MILU) and specialized reasoning (e.g., 98.3% structural biology MC). In agentic tests, it scored 91.0% on ChartXiv (with tools) and 87.6% on ScreenSpot-Pro (with tools), while demonstrating superior safety improvements, such as an ASR of 2.34% in coding red-teaming, and an achieved final balance of $10,937 in Vending-Bench.

When evaluating multilingual capabilities, the **Multilingual Massive Multitask Language Understanding (MMLU)** benchmark extends the standard MMLU evaluation across 42 languages, spanning diverse language families and resource levels, ranging from high-resource languages like French and German to low-resource languages such as Yoruba, Igbo, and Chichewa. Additionally, **MILU** focuses specifically on 10 Indic languages (Bengali, Gujarati, Hindi, Kannada, Malayalam, Marathi, Odia, Punjabi, Tamil, and Telugu) alongside English, testing culturally grounded knowledge comprehension. The **INCLUDE** benchmark covers 44 languages, drawing questions from regional academic and professional examinations, thereby emphasizing in-language and in-culture knowledge rather than merely translated content. All models were evaluated using structured JSON output. Specifically, Gemini 3.1 Pro was evaluated using dynamic thinking at its default high level. GPT-5.4 was evaluated with reasoning effort set to high. Claude Opus 4.7 was evaluated with adaptive thinking enabled. Claude Opus 4.6 and Claude Sonnet 4.6 were evaluated with adaptive thinking on INCLUDE and a medium extended-thinking budget on GMMLU and MILU.

### GMMLU Results
Claude Opus 4.7 achieved an average accuracy of 89.9% across all evaluated languages. The detailed scores show the following: For the English benchmark, Claude Opus 4.7 achieved 93.4% accuracy. In the high-resource average, Claude Opus 4.7 scored 91.5% (outperforming Claude Opus 4.6’s 90.7%, Claude Sonnet 4.6’s 89.2%, Gemini 3.1 Pro’s 93.1%, and GPT-5.4’s 91.5%). In the mid-resource average, Claude Opus 4.7 scored 91.1%, which exceeded Claude Opus 4.6’s 89.6%, Claude Sonnet 4.6’s 88.2%, Gemini 3.1 Pro’s 92.9%, and GPT-5.4’s 91.4%. For the low-resource average, Claude Opus 4.7 scored 86.2%. Individual low-resource language scores include: Igbo (81.3% for Opus 4.7 vs. 70.1% for Claude Opus 4.6), Yoruba (82.9% for Opus 4.7 vs. 70.8% for Claude Opus 4.6), Somali (84.1% for Opus 4.7 vs. 72.0% for Claude Opus 4.6), Malagasy (84.8% for Opus 4.7 vs. 78.6% for Claude Opus 4.6), Chichewa (84.9% for Opus 4.7 vs. 71.2% for Claude Opus 4.6). Overall, the overall average accuracy for all languages is 89.9%, with a gap to the English score of -3.6%. The best low-resource score is Chichewa (84.9%).

### MILU Results
Claude Opus 4.7 achieved an average accuracy of 89.9% across all evaluated languages for the Multi-task Indic Language Understanding benchmark. Specific language scores include: Malayalam (87.3% for Opus 4.7 vs. 85.3% for Claude Opus 4.6), Punjabi (87.7% for Opus 4.7 vs. 85.6% for Claude Opus 4.6), Odia (87.8% for Opus 4.7 vs. 85.3% for Claude Opus 4.6), Tamil (89.0% for Opus 4.7 vs. 85.9% for Claude Opus 4.6), Marathi (89.5% for Opus 4.7 vs. 86.8% for Claude Opus 4.6), and Gujarati (89.7% for Opus 4.7 vs. 87.2% for Claude Opus 4.6). The overall average accuracy for all languages is 89.9%, with a gap to the English score of -2.4%. The best performing single language is Hindi (93.0%).

### INCLUDE Results
Claude Opus 4.7 achieved an average accuracy of 87.0% across all evaluated languages for the INCLUDE benchmark. Specific language scores include: German (69.8% for Opus 4.7 vs. 71.2% for Claude Opus 4.6), Turkish (70.2% for Opus 4.7 vs. 71.2% for Claude Opus 4.6), Urdu (74.5% for Opus 4.7 vs. 72.8% for Claude Opus 4.6), Uzbek (79.1% for Opus 4.7 vs. 77.9% for Claude Opus 4.6), Telugu (79.5% for Opus 4.7 vs. 77.1% for Claude Opus 4.6), and Nepali (80.1% for Opus 4.7 vs. 78.5% for Claude Opus 4.6). The overall average accuracy for all languages is 87.0%, with a gap to the English score of -2.7%.

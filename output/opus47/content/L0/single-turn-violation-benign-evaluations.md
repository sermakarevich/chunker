# single-turn-violation-benign-evaluations

**Parent:** [[content/L1/claude-opus-4-7-cyber-safety-benchmarks|claude-opus-4-7-cyber-safety-benchmarks]] — Claude Opus 4.7's cyber capabilities are generally similar to Opus 4.6, achieving a pass@1 of 96% on a 35-challenge subset of Cybench, but falling below Claude Mythos Preview in CyberGym and Firefox 147 exploitation. While the model achieved the best overall score in high-difficulty benign requests, its harmless response rate dropped approximately one percentage point below Opus 4.6, and it showed a critical 22% failure rate (reduced to 11% via system prompt mitigations) when providing appropriate responses concerning illegal and controlled substances.

Anthropic's single-turn violative request evaluation results across multiple languages demonstrate the model's harmless response rate (higher numbers are better). For the 'with thinking' mode, Claude Opus 4.6 achieved the highest harmless response rate at 99.71%, followed by Claude Opus 4.7 at 97.93%, and Claude Mythos Preview at 97.97%. When Claude Opus 4.7 was evaluated against Claude Opus 4.6, the Opus 4.7 model scored approximately one percentage point lower on the overall harmless response rate than Claude Opus 4.6. This difference was most noticeable for responses run with thinking mode. Differences between the tested languages (Arabic, English, French, Hindi, Korean, Mandarin Chinese, and Russian) were minimal. The difference in scores is largely attributed to Opus 4.7's responses concerning illegal and controlled substances, where Opus 4.7 failed to provide an appropriate response 22% of the time, which is significantly higher than the less than 5% failure rate observed for Claude Opus 4.6. Additionally, Opus 4.7, even when using thinking mode, sometimes provides overly-specific answers about safer use in the context of harm reduction. While the line between harm reduction and enablement is difficult to draw, Anthropic's System prompt mitigations applied on Claude.ai have proven effective in reducing the failure rate in this category from 22% to 11%.

In benign request evaluations (where the refusal rate is measured, and lower is better), Anthropic's single-turn benign request evaluation results show the following rates:

*   **Claude Opus 4.7:** Achieved an overall refusal rate of 0.28% (± 0.04%) without thinking, 0.50% (± 0.08%) with thinking, and 0.06% (± 0.02%). In Arabic, the rate was 0.34%; in English, it was 0.22%; in French, it was 0.28%; in Hindi, it was 0.27%; in Korean, it was 0.34%; and in Russian, it was 0.27%. A rate of 0.22% was recorded for English requests.
*   **Claude Mythos Preview:** Registered an overall refusal rate of 0.06% (± 0.02%) without thinking, 0.09% (± 0.03%) with thinking, and 0.02% (± 0.01%).
*   **Claude Sonnet 4.6:** Showed an overall refusal rate of 0.41% (± 0.05%) without thinking, 0.48% (± 0.08%) with thinking, and 0.35% (± 0.07%).
*   **Claude Opus 4.6:** Maintained an overall refusal rate of 0.71% (± 0.07%) without thinking, 0.85% (± 0.11%) with thinking, and 0.58% (± 0.09%).

Anthropic's single-turn benign request evaluation results by language show these rates for the languages Arabic, English, French, Hindi, Korean, Mandarin Chinese, and Russian:

*   **Claude Opus 4.7:** Rates were 0.34% in Arabic, 0.22% in English, 0.42% in Chinese, 0.22% in French, 0.28% in Korean, 0.27% in Russian, and 0.34% in Hindi.
*   **Claude Mythos Preview:** Rates were 0.03% in Arabic, 0.05% in English, 0.08% in Chinese, 0.04% in French, 0.08% in Korean, 0.05% in Russian, and 0.06% in Hindi.
*   **Claude Sonnet 4.6:** Rates were 0.25% in Arabic, 0.49% in English, 0.37% in Chinese, 0.24% in French, 0.43% in Korean, 0.27% in Russian, and 0.83% in Hindi.
*   **Claude Opus 4.6:** Rates were 0.39% in Arabic, 1.09% in English, 0.57% in Chinese, 0.61% in French, 0.81% in Korean, 0.40% in Russian, and 1.11% in Hindi.

In higher-difficulty violative request evaluations, the harmless response rate (higher numbers are better) for the English-only prompts were: Claude Opus 4.7: 99.05% (± 0.11%), 99.16% (± 0.15%), and 98.94% (± 0.17%). Claude Mythos Preview: 99.14% (± 0.11%), 99.28% (± 0.14%), and 99.01% (± 0.16%). Claude Sonnet 4.6: 99.27% (± 0.10%), 99.14% (± 0.15%), and 99.40% (± 0.13%). Claude Opus 4.6: 99.19% (± 0.11%), 99.09% (± 0.16%), and 99.28% (± 0.14%).

In higher-difficulty benign request evaluations, the rates of over-refusal (lower is better) for the English-only prompts were: Claude Opus 4.7: 0.01% (± 0.01%), 0.02% (± 0.02%), and 0.00%. Claude Mythos Preview: 0.02% (± 0.02%), 0.03% (± 0.03%), and 0.01% (± 0.01%). Claude Sonnet 4.6: 0.16% (± 0.05%), 0.19% (± 0.07%), and 0.14% (± 0.06%). Claude Opus 4.6: 0.04% (± 0.02%), 0.06% (± 0.04%), and 0.03% (± 0.03%).

These evaluation results, including the analysis of single-turn violative, benign, and high-difficulty requests, reveal that Claude Opus 4.7 performed well, noting that Opus 4.7 posted the best overall scores in the higher-difficulty benign request evaluation, with a near-zero undesired refusal rate. While Opus 4.7 generally performed well, the model struggled significantly with illegal and controlled substances, exhibiting a 22% failure rate when providing appropriate responses, compared to Claude Opus 4.6's rate of less than 5%. Additionally, the use of System prompt mitigations on Claude.ai successfully reduced Opus 4.7's failure rate in this specific area from 22% to 11%.

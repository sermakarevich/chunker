# safety-and-alignment-audits

**Parent:** [[index]]

The comprehensive assessment of Claude Opus 4.7 involved synthesizing evaluations of its technical safety, ethical alignment, internal emotional representations, and general behavioral consistency. The results indicate that while Opus 4.7 exhibits strong general alignment and improved technical metrics, its overall profile suggests developmental areas for improvement, particularly regarding the depth of its self-understanding and its capacity for independent reflection. 

### **Technical Safety and Behavioral Audits**

Opus 4.7's safety and robustness were rigorously tested across specialized benchmarks. In the **Claude Code Evaluation**, 1,220 total tests were executed, covering both malicious and dual-use/benign scenarios, derived from 61 distinct malicious topics and 61 complementary dual-use/benign queries. The model showed marked improvements over Claude Opus 4.6, achieving a measurable refusal rate of 91.15% in the malicious category, which significantly surpassed the 82.21% recorded by Opus 4.6. However, its refusal rate for dual-use and benign tasks remained at 91.83%, which was notably lower than the 98.61% achieved by Opus 4.6 in this specific domain.

In the **Computer Use Simulations** evaluation, which covered 112 total tasks run 224 times (with and without thinking enabled) using the Opus 4.5 version, Opus 4.7 achieved a Refusal Rate of 89.29% when thinking was enabled, exceeding Claude Opus 4.6's 87.05%. 

Advanced red-teaming was conducted using the **Shade tool** for both coding and computer use environments. The **Coding Environment Results** showed substantial improvements: without safeguards, the Attack Success Rate (ASR) for 1 attempt dropped from 25.92% (Opus 4.6) to 2.34% for Opus 4.7. At 200 attempts, the rate fell from 97.5% (Opus 4.6) to 60.0% with adaptive thinking. Crucially, applying additional user-protective safeguards reduced the ASR further to 0.43% at 1 attempt and 25.0% at 200 attempts. Similarly, in the **Computer Use Environment Results**, with adaptive thinking and without safeguards, the ASR dropped from 78.6% (Opus 4.6) to 0.46% for Opus 4.7 at 1 attempt, and from 50.0% to 7.14% at 200 attempts. In the **Browser Use Robustness** assessment, Opus 4.7 achieved a consistent per-environment ASR of 54.05% without safeguards, regardless of thinking status, a result much better than Opus 4.6’s 86.49% without thinking. When deploying safeguards, Opus 4.7's ASR improved markedly to 0.75% (without thinking) and 0.74% (with thinking), matching Claude Mythos Preview’s performance.

# claude-code-bibliography

**Parent:** [[index]]

The provided text is a bibliography and reference list for a study analyzing the architecture of Claude Code, an agentic coding tool by Anthropic. The bibliography includes academic papers, technical blogs, and documentation from 2023 to 2026 that inform the study's analysis of agentic systems, memory, safety, and multi-agent coordination. 

Key references include:
- **Agentic Architecture and Design**: Lance Martin's "Agent design patterns" (2026) and the Anthropic Research blog post "Building effective agents" by Erik Schluntz and Barry Zhang (2024). The study also references the LLM-as-OS framing popularized by Andrej Karpathy's 2023 talk and the MemGPT work by Charles Packer et al. (2023).
- **Safety and Governance**: The European Commission's GPAI Code of Practice (2025) and the EU AI Act (2026), as well as the International AI Safety Report (2026) and the MIT AI Agent Index (2026) by Leon Staufer et al.
- **Context and Memory**: Work on agentic context engineering by Qizheng Zhang et al. (2025a), memory mechanisms in foundation agents by Wei-Chieh Huang et al. (2026), and a survey on LLM-based agent memory by Zeyu Zhang et al. (2025b).
- **Coding Agents and Benchmarks**: Research on SWE-bench by Carlos E Jimenez et al. (2023) and the SWE-agent system by John Yang et al. (2024). The list also includes an empirical study by Hao He et al. (2025) on how Cursor AI affects short-term velocity and long-term complexity in open-source projects.
- **Multi-Agent Frameworks**: MetaGPT (Sirui Hong et al., 2023), AutoGen (Qingyun Wu et al., 2024), and ChatDev (Chen Qian et al., 2024).
- **Proactivity and Human-AI Interaction**: Research on proactive conversational agents by Xingyu Bruce Liu et al. (2025) and the trade-offs of proactive programming support by Kevin Pu et al. (2025).
- **Model Context Protocol (MCP)**: The study references the Model Context Protocol landscape and security threats analyzed by Xinyi Hou et al. (2025) and the official announcement of the Agentic AI Foundation (AAIF) by The Linux Foundation (2025).
- **Persistence and Recovery**: The study references the research on long-horizon software tasks by Thomas Kwa et al. and the AI scientist pipeline by Chris Lu et al. (2024).

Additionally, the bibliography lists specific software repositories and tools used for architectural comparison, such as OpenClaw (Peter Steinberger and contributors, 2026) and LangGraph (LangChain, Inc., 2024).

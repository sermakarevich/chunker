# debugging-strategies-and-mindset

**Parent:** [[content/L1/developer-productivity-and-workflow|developer-productivity-and-workflow]] — Software development requires mastery of editor fluency for text manipulation, utilizing VCS for comprehensive project management, and employing systematic debugging techniques like 'chop' and tracing statements to identify bugs efficiently.

Debugging is a process where software developers find and fix errors, known as bugs. Although the term 'bug' is ancient—dating back to a reported incident involving a moth caught in a relay in an early computer system, and recorded by technician Sophocles in Ajax—the general principle remains: modern computer systems are limited to performing only what programmers instruct them to do, not necessarily what users desire. Since no one writes perfect software, developers must expect that debugging will occupy a major portion of their workday. When approaching debugging, developers should treat it as general problem-solving, and they must avoid attributing blame; the focus must remain solely on fixing the problem.

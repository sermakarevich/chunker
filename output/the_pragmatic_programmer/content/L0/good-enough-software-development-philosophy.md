**Good-Enough Software**

The difficulty of achieving perfect software quality is illustrated by an old joke: A manufacturer placed an order for 100,000 Integrated Circuits (ICs) with a specified defect rate of one chip in 10,000. After a few weeks, the order arrived in a large box containing thousands of ICs, and a small box containing just ten. The small box was labeled, “These are the faulty ones.” The text notes that the real world makes it nearly impossible to produce much software that is truly perfect, especially software that is bug-free. Time, technology, and temperament conspire against perfect creation. However, the text suggests this does not have to be frustrating.

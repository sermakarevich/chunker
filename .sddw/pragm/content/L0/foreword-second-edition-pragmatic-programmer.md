# foreword-second-edition-pragmatic-programmer

**Parent:** [[content/L1/pragmatic-programmer-philosophy-and-craftsmanship|pragmatic-programmer-philosophy-and-craftsmanship]] — The book defines a Pragmatic Programmer as an attitude that requires considering all problems within their larger context, emphasizing continuous, small improvements (*Kaizen*) and asserting that individual craftsmanship is vital even in large engineering projects. It is structured as self-contained topics that readers can follow in any order and includes exercises, challenges, and a detailed appendix.

In the Foreword, the author recounts watching the coding community react with excitement to the news of the second edition of *The Pragmatic Programmer* twenty years after the first publication. The author, having read an unreleased copy, observes that calling the book merely a 'technical book' does a disservice, because technical books often intimidate readers with big words, obscure terms, and convoluted examples. The authors praise Dave Thomas and Andy Hunt for conquering the difficult challenge of writing in a way that matches the excitement felt by people who are just learning the lessons. The authors do not talk down to the reader, nor do they assume the reader is an expert or has read the first edition; instead, the authors treat all readers as programmers who merely want to get better and provide actionable steps throughout the book.

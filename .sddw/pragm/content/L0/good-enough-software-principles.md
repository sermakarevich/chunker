The following text details the technical concept of software entropy and related design principles. Before discussing Topic 5, Good-Enough Software, the book references Topic 1, It’s Your Life, and Topic 38, Programming by Coincidence.

**CHALLENGES**

While reviewing a draft of the first edition, John Lakos raised the following question: When attempting to catalyze change, how can one determine whether the result is akin to making stone soup or frog soup? The question asks whether the decision regarding the resulting soup is subjective or objective. The author then suggests an exercise in situational awareness, asking for quick, non-visual answers to questions such as: how many lights are in the ceiling above the person; how many exits are in the room; and how many people are present. The author notes that this exercise is practiced by individuals ranging from Boy and Girl Scouts to Navy SEALs, advising the reader to develop the habit of genuinely observing surroundings and applying that same careful attention to a software project.

**Topic 5: Good-Enough Software**

This section addresses the reality that producing software that is truly perfect, particularly one that is bug-free, is often impossible due to the combined influence of time, technology, and temperament. A joke illustrates this point: a manufacturer placed an order for 100,000 Integrated Circuits (ICs) with a specified defect rate of one faulty chip in 10,000.

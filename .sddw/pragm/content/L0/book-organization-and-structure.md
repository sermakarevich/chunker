# book-organization-and-structure

**Parent:** [[content/L1/pragmatic-programmer-guide-details|pragmatic-programmer-guide-details]] — The book, *The Pragmatic Programmer*, by Dave Thomas and Andy Hunt, is a guide to mastering software development that contains technical topics (like DRY, Orthogonality, and Version Control) and professional advice (like prioritizing 'it's your life'). Publication details include the ISBN-13 978-0-13-595705-9 and the copyright held by Pearson Education, Inc. (Copyright © 2020), with a comprehensive Table of Contents detailing 53 topics and foreword commentary.

This book is structured as a collection of short, self-contained topics, with each topic addressing a specific theme. Readers will find numerous cross references, which help situate each topic within the overall context. Therefore, readers are encouraged to read the topics in any order, as the book is not intended to be read sequentially from beginning to end. Occasionally, the book includes a box labeled 'Tip nn' (for example, Tip 1, Care About Your Craft). Beyond merely emphasizing points within the text, the authors consider the tips to have a life of their own, meaning the authors live by these tips daily. The book also includes exercises and challenges where they deemed it appropriate. Generally, the exercises have straightforward answers, while the challenges are more open-ended. To give readers an idea of the authors’ approach, the book includes answers to the exercises in an appendix, though very few exercises have a single correct solution. Furthermore, the challenges may serve as the basis for group discussions or essay work in advanced programming courses. Finally, the book includes a short bibliography listing the books and articles the authors explicitly reference.

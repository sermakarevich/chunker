# multilingual-nlp-resources

**Parent:** [[blocks/advanced-rag-architectures-and-benchmarks|The synthesis details advanced RAG frameworks, including Hierarchical Agentic RAG, Corrective RAG, and GEAR, alongside rigorous evaluation using benchmarks like HotpotQA, BEIR, MS MARCO, and specialized tools like FlashRAG and Bergen; it also covers foundational knowledge from datasets such as SQuAD (2016) and FEVER (2018).]]

The provided list of academic and technical resources includes works related to multilingual natural language processing (NLP) and advanced language model training.

**Multilingual Relevance Assessment:** One contribution is the paper "knowing when you don’t know": A multilingual relevance assessment dataset for robust retrieval-augmented generation," authored by Nandan Thakur, Luiz Bonifacio, Xinyu Zhang, Odunayo Ogundepo, Ehsan Kamalloo, David Alfonso-Hermelo, Xiaoguang Li, Qun Liu, Boxing Chen, Mehdi Rezagholizadeh, and Jimmy Lin. This work is dated 2024.

**Language Modeling Architectures:** Another included resource details "Pointer sentinel mixture models," authored by Stephen Merity, Caiming Xiong, James Bradbury, and Richard Socher, from 2016.

**Knowledge and Reasoning:** For mathematical reasoning, the paper "Training verifiers to solve math word problems" was published in 2021 by Karl Cobbe, Vineet Kosaraju, Mohammad Bavarian, Mark Chen, Heewoo Jun, Lukasz Kaiser, Matthias Plappert, Jerry Tworek, Jacob Hilton, Reiichiro Nakano, Christopher Hesse, and John Schulman.

**Parallel Corpora:** Finally, the resource "The JRC-Acquis: A multilingual aligned parallel corpus with 20+ languages" was published in May 2006 by Ralf Steinberger, Bruno Pouliquen, Anna Widiger, Camelia Ignat, Tomaž Erjavec, Dan Tufiş, and Dániel Varga, appearing in the proceedings of the Fifth International Conference on Language Resources and Evaluation (LREC‘06) in Genoa, Italy.

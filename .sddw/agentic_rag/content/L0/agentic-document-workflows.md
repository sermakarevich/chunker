Agentic Document Workflows (ADW) extend traditional Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) paradigms by enabling end-to-end knowledge work automation. These ADW workflows orchestrate complex document-centric processes, integrating document parsing, retrieval, reasoning, and structured outputs using intelligent agents. ADW systems improve upon the limitations of Intelligent Document Processing (IDP) and RAG by maintaining state, coordinating multi-step workflows, and applying domain-specific logic to documents. The typical ADW workflow proceeds through the following five stages: 1. Document Parsing and Information Structuring: Enterprise-grade tools, such as LlamaParse, are used to parse documents and extract specific data fields. These fields include invoice numbers, dates, vendor information, line items, and payment terms. The resulting structured data is then organized for subsequent processing. 2. State Maintenance Across Processes: The system maintains state regarding document context, which ensures consistency and relevance throughout multi-step workflows. Furthermore, the system tracks the progression of the document through various processing stages. 3. Knowledge Retrieval: The system retrieves relevant references from external knowledge bases, such as LlamaCloud, or from vector indexes. This process also retrieves real-time, domain-specific guidelines, which enhances decision-making. 4. Agentic Orchestration: Intelligent agents apply specific business rules and perform multi-hop reasoning. These agents are responsible for generating actionable recommendations and orchestrating components, including parsers, retrievers, and external APIs, ensuring seamless integration. 5. Actionable Output Generation: The outputs are presented in structured formats customized for specific use cases. The agents synthesize recommendations and extracted insights into concise and actionable reports.

For example, in an Invoice Payments Workflow, a user submits a prompt to generate a payment recommendation report based on a submitted invoice and the associated vendor contract terms. Following the ADW system process: First, the system parses the invoice to extract key details such as the invoice number, date, vendor information, line items, and payment terms. Second, the system retrieves the corresponding vendor contract to verify payment terms and identify any applicable discounts or compliance requirements. Third, the system generates a payment recommendation report, which includes the original amount due, potential early payment discounts, budget impact analysis, and strategic payment actions. The integrated response provided for an example invoice (INV-2025-045 for $15,000.00) details that an early payment discount of 2% is available if paid by 2025-04-10, reducing the amount due to $14,700.00. Furthermore, a bulk order discount of 5% was applied because the subtotal exceeded $10,000.00. The final recommendation was to approve early payment to save 2% and ensure timely fund allocation for upcoming project phases.

Key features and advantages of ADW include: State Maintenance, which tracks document context and workflow stage to ensure process consistency; Multi-Step Orchestration, which manages complex workflows involving multiple components and external tools; Domain-Specific Intelligence, which applies tailored business rules and guidelines for precise recommendations; Scalability, which supports large-scale document processing through modular and dynamic agent integration; and Enhanced Productivity, which automates repetitive tasks while augmenting human expertise in decision-making.

The comparative table highlights that ADW focuses on document-centric, end-to-end workflows, maintaining state across multi-step processes, and deeply integrating domain-specific business rules and tools, making it ideal for multi-domain enterprise workflows, such as contract review, invoice processing, and claims analysis. While ADW can incur resource overhead and requires domain standardization, it offers advantages over Traditional RAG and Agentic RAG by providing structured reasoning across documents and facilitating end-to-end automation, exemplified by its use in contract review, invoice processing, and claims analysis.

Agentic RAG applications demonstrate transformative potential across multiple sectors. In Customer Support, Agentic RAG systems enhance services by dynamically retrieving relevant information and generating personalized responses, such as when Twitch leveraged an agentic workflow on Amazon Bedrock to generate detailed ad proposals by retrieving advertiser data, historical campaign performance, and audience demographics. In Healthcare, Agentic RAG assists clinicians by integrating patient-specific data, such as electronic health records (EHR) and up-to-date medical literature, to generate comprehensive patient case summaries and personalized care recommendations. For Legal and Contract Analysis, an Agentic RAG system uses semantic search capabilities and legal knowledge graphs to analyze contracts, extract critical clauses, and identify potential risks. In Finance and Risk Analysis, these systems transform the industry by using real-time data streams and predictive modeling, such as in auto insurance claims processing, where the system retrieves policy details and combines them with accident data to generate recommendations while ensuring regulatory compliance.
